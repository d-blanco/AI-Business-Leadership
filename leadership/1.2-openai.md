# Business Leadership with OpenAI

As a business leader, your success with AI depends not on technical implementation, but on how effectively you can articulate strategy, frame problems, and leverage AI as a strategic tool. This lab introduces AI-assisted workflow for executives creating business proposals, presentations, and strategic communications.

You will use free AI tools to develop a complete business initiative proposal — from branding to executive presentation to marketing assets. The goal is to experience AI as a strategic partner while understanding its limitations, risks, and data privacy implications in an enterprise context.

---

## Key Concepts for Leaders

#### Prompt Engineering
The skill of providing clear, detailed instructions to AI to get useful outputs. A vague prompt yields generic results; a strategic prompt with business context yields actionable insights.

#### Iterative Refinement
AI outputs are rarely perfect on the first try. The best results come from reviewing outputs and refining your prompts based on what's missing or incorrect.

#### AI Hallucinations
When AI generates information that sounds confident but is factually incorrect or completely fabricated. Critical for leadership to verify all data, metrics, and claims before using them in actual business decisions.

#### Data Privacy
Where does your data go when you use free AI tools? Understanding data handling is essential when working with sensitive business information, customer data, or proprietary strategy.

#### AI as Tool, Not Replacement
AI accelerates and augments decision-making but requires human judgment, domain expertise, and ethical oversight. Leaders who understand AI's capabilities and limitations will make better strategic decisions.

## Part 1 — Create Your Company Brand

Before presenting a business initiative, you need a company context. In this section, you'll create a fictional financial or technology company and develop its brand identity using AI.

### Step 1: Create a Free OpenAI Account

1. Go to [chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com) and create a free account.
2. Log in and familiarize yourself with the ChatGPT interface.

> **Important:** Free AI tools like ChatGPT do not guarantee data privacy or confidentiality. Assume anything you enter could be stored, analyzed, or used for model training. Never use real customer data, proprietary information, or sensitive business details in free tools.

### Step 2: Choose or Create Your Company

You can either:
- **Option A:** Use the sample company **Genera Financial** (a digital-first investment platform targeting millennials and Gen Z)
- **Option B:** Create your own financial or tech company

If creating your own, consider these sample company ideas:
- **Apex Trading** — zero-commission stock trading platform
- **NovaBank** — AI-powered digital banking for small businesses  
- **VaultTech** — cryptocurrency custody and security solutions
- **Horizon Wealth** — automated investment advisor for retirement planning
- **PayFlow** — instant payment processing for e-commerce

### Step 3: Generate a Logo

Use ChatGPT's image generation capability (DALL-E) to create a company logo.

**Sample Prompt:**
```
Create a modern, professional logo for a financial technology company called [Your Company Name]. 

Style: Clean, minimalist, trustworthy
Colors: Blues and greens to convey security and growth
Format: Icon + company name

The logo should work well on both light and dark backgrounds and convey innovation and reliability.
```

> **Tip:** If you're not satisfied with the result, refine your prompt. Be specific about what you want changed: "Make the icon simpler," "Use a darker blue," "Make the text bolder," etc.

If you lack creativity or want to save time, you can use the provided sample file **genera-logo.png** in your workspace.

## Part 3 — Create an Executive Presentation

Now you'll use AI to draft a strategic presentation for your leadership team.

### Requirements:

- **6 slides total**
- **Audience:** Executive leadership team (CEO, CFO, Board)
- **Tone:** Professional, strategic, data-driven
- **Objective:** Get approval and budget allocation for your initiative

### Slide Structure:

1. **Title Slide** — Initiative name, your role, date
2. **Executive Summary** — One-slide overview of the opportunity
3. **Business Case** — Why this initiative matters now (market trends, competitive pressure)
4. **Financial Impact** — Revenue, costs, customer metrics, ROI timeline
5. **Risks & Mitigation** — What could go wrong and how you'll address it
6. **Recommendation & Next Steps** — Clear ask and action plan

### Prompt Template:

Use a structured prompt like the one below. **Do not copy-paste this verbatim** — customize it with your company, initiative, and metrics.

```
Create a 6-slide PowerPoint presentation for an executive leadership team.

Company: [Your company name and brief description]
Initiative: [Your chosen initiative]

Slide 1 — Title Slide
- Initiative name
- Presented by: [Your name], Chief Strategy Officer
- Date: March 2026

Slide 2 — Executive Summary
- Brief overview of the initiative
- Core value proposition
- Expected impact in one sentence

Slide 3 — Strategic Rationale
- Why this initiative is important now
- Market trends supporting the decision
- Competitive landscape considerations

Slide 4 — Financial Impact
- Current customer base: [number]
- Projected new customers: [number]
- Revenue impact: [amount]
- Customer acquisition cost: [amount]
- Break-even timeline: [months]
- 3-year net impact: [amount]

Slide 5 — Risk Assessment & Mitigation
- Key risks (regulatory, operational, market)
- How we will mitigate each risk
- Dependencies and assumptions

Slide 6 — Recommendation & Next Steps
- Clear recommendation: Approve budget of $[X]M
- Phase 1 timeline and milestones
- Success metrics and checkpoints

Tone: Professional, strategic, confident but realistic
Format: Provide slide titles and 3-5 concise bullet points per slide

Before proceeding, do you have any questions?
```

### Understanding AI Limitations

> **Important:** ChatGPT's free version cannot generate actual PowerPoint files. It will provide you with text content for slides that you must then manually create in PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Keynote.

**However**, the paid version of ChatGPT Plus can generate and export presentations in certain formats. More importantly, **Microsoft 365 Copilot** (an add-on subscription for Microsoft 365) allows you to use AI **directly inside PowerPoint** to:
- Generate slide decks from prompts or documents
- Reformat and redesign slides
- Summarize long presentations
- Add speaker notes automatically

> **Your lab environment does not include Microsoft 365 Copilot**, but you should be aware of its capabilities as this is where enterprise AI tooling is heading. Organizations with Microsoft 365 can integrate AI into their daily workflows without sending data to external tools like ChatGPT.

### Alternative Tools (Free or Freemium):

If you want to accelerate slide creation, try these tools:

- **Google Slides** — Use ChatGPT to generate content, then manually build slides in Google Slides (free with Google account)
- **Canva** — Free tier includes presentation templates; Canva's AI features (like "Magic Write") can help draft slide content
- **Gamma.app** — AI-powered presentation builder (free tier available) that generates slides from text prompts
- **Beautiful.ai** — AI-assisted slide design (free trial available)

For this lab, focus on **the quality of your prompt and the strategic thinking**, not the visual polish. If you only have time to create text-based slides, that's acceptable.

## Part 4 — Create Marketing Assets

Once your initiative is approved (hypothetically), you'll need to communicate it to customers, partners, and stakeholders.

### Step 1: Generate a Marketing Image

Use ChatGPT's DALL-E image generator to create a promotional image for your initiative.

**Sample Prompt:**
```
Create a professional marketing image for a [brief description of your initiative].

Visual style: Modern, clean, aspirational
Mood: Trustworthy and innovative
Text overlay: "[Initiative tagline]"

The image should work for social media, email campaigns, and website banners.
```

For example, if your initiative is "Removing Trading Fees," your tagline might be:
- "Trade Free. Grow Faster."
- "Zero Fees. Infinite Potential."
- "Investing Without Limits."

### Step 2: Generate Supporting Copy

Ask ChatGPT to draft:
- **Email announcement** to existing customers (3-4 paragraphs)
- **Social media posts** for LinkedIn and Twitter (short, engaging)
- **Website banner headline and subheadline**

**Sample Prompt:**
```
Write a customer email announcement for [your initiative].

Audience: Existing customers who are already engaged with our platform
Tone: Excited but professional, emphasizing customer benefit
Length: 3-4 paragraphs

Include:
- Clear explanation of the change
- Why we're doing this
- What it means for them
- Call to action

Do not use overly promotional language. Keep it genuine and customer-focused.
```

## Part 5 — Data Visualization Challenge (Optional — Stretch Goal)

If you have time, create data visualizations to support your business case.

### Step 1: Prepare Data

Ask ChatGPT to generate sample data for your initiative. For example:

```
Create a CSV dataset showing projected monthly revenue for the next 24 months after launching [your initiative].

Include columns for:
- Month (1-24)
- Revenue from existing customers
- Revenue from new customers
- Total revenue
- Costs (fixed and variable)
- Net profit

Base the projections on the following assumptions:
[Paste your metrics from Part 2]

Format the output as a clean CSV I can copy-paste into Excel.
```

### Step 2: Visualize in Excel or Google Sheets

Paste the CSV data into a spreadsheet and create:
- **Line chart** showing revenue growth over 24 months
- **Bar chart** comparing costs vs. revenue by quarter
- **Pie chart** showing revenue breakdown by customer segment

### Step 3: Understand AI-Powered Spreadsheet Tools

**Microsoft 365 Copilot** can automate this entire process inside Excel:
- Analyze data and suggest chart types
- Generate pivot tables automatically
- Create formulas based on natural language prompts ("Calculate year-over-year growth")
- Summarize trends in plain English

**Alternatives you can try:**
- **Google Sheets + Gemini** — Google's AI integration in Workspace (if available in your account)
- **ChatGPT + Manual Charting** — Ask ChatGPT to suggest chart types and formulas, then manually create them
- **Notion AI** — If you use Notion for docs, its AI can help structure and summarize data tables

> **Learning objective:** AI can accelerate data analysis and visualization, but you still need to understand what story the data tells. Don't blindly trust AI-generated insights — verify the logic and check for errors.

## Part 6 — Critical Reflection

Now that you've created a complete business initiative with AI assistance, reflect on the following questions:

### 1. Hallucinations and Accuracy
- Review the content ChatGPT generated. Did it invent any facts, statistics, or claims that weren't in your prompt?
- What information would you **need to verify** before presenting this to real executives?
- If ChatGPT suggested market trends or competitor data, where would you validate that information?

### 2. Data Privacy and Risk
- Where is the data you entered stored? (Hint: OpenAI's servers, not your company's infrastructure)
- Did you use any sensitive or proprietary information? If this were a real company, what data would you **never** put into ChatGPT?
- How would your company's legal, security, or compliance teams react if they knew you used free AI tools for strategic planning?

### 3. Quality vs. Speed
- How much faster was this process compared to creating everything manually?
- Did the AI output meet your quality standards, or did it require significant editing?
- What parts of the process **required human judgment** that AI couldn't replace?

### 4. Enterprise AI vs. Free Tools
- Microsoft 365 Copilot keeps data within your organization's Microsoft tenant and doesn't use your data for model training (per enterprise agreements).
- Free ChatGPT may retain data and improve models based on user inputs (check [OpenAI's data usage policies](https://openai.com/policies/usage-policies)).
- What's the business value of paying for enterprise AI tools vs. using free alternatives?

## Conclusion

This lab demonstrated how business leaders can use AI as a strategic tool to accelerate decision-making, content creation, and communication — without needing technical skills. However, it also highlighted AI's limitations: hallucinations, data privacy risks, and the irreplaceable need for human judgment.

As AI becomes embedded in enterprise workflows, leaders who understand both its capabilities and constraints will make better decisions. Free tools like ChatGPT are useful for learning and brainstorming, but production business workflows require enterprise-grade AI with data governance, compliance, and security controls.

The next generation of business tools (Microsoft 365 Copilot, Google Workspace AI, Salesforce Einstein) will integrate AI directly into the platforms you already use — keeping data secure while accelerating strategic work. Understanding how to prompt, refine, and critically evaluate AI outputs is now a core leadership skill.
